Written Exam Exemptions

Who is Exempt?
  • Qualified Military Pilots — IAF, Indian Navy, Indian Army, or Indian Coast Guard pilots with at least 1500 hours of flying experience.
  • Holders of a Valid RTR(R) Certificate & Licence — Person who already holds a valid Radio Telephone Operator's (Restricted) Licence.
  • Holders of Equivalent Foreign Licences — Valid FRTOL or equivalent issued by Commonwealth countries or the Philippines.
Exempted candidates must apply through the eGCA portal for the written exam exemption grant. After approval, they may directly apply for the Practical Examination. Exemption is valid for 3 years from the grant date. All exempted candidates also get 3 practical attempts.

Exam Syllabus

REG Regulations & Procedure
▶
01International Telecommunication Convention and Radio Regulations.
02General and Aeronautical 'Q' Code signals and other abbreviations as contained in Annex 10 (Vol. I & II) of ICAO.
03General Radio-telephone communication procedure and radio telephone communication procedure for distress, urgency and direction finding.
04Procedure for distress communication in maritime mobile service.
05Words and figures spelling used in radio telephony.
06Licensing requirement of installation and operation of radio apparatus used in aircraft.
07Minimum requirement of radio equipment to be carried on aircraft as prescribed in Annexure 6 of the ICAO and DGCA in India.
08FIR in India and main Radio communication and Navigation facilities available, together with principal frequencies to be used for communication and navigation within India.
09Meteorological codes, Pre-Flight briefing service, and their usages.
10Knowledge of notices to airmen issued by the Civil Aviation Authorities in India, as applicable to the Aeronautical mobile and Air traffic control services.
RADIO Radio Principles & Practice
▶
01Electrical units such as Volt, Ampere, Ohm, Watt, Wavelength, Frequency, and their relationship.
02Radio frequency propagation — day and night frequencies, skip distance fading, ground shadow and its effect on communication; choice of frequencies for maximum efficiency in air-ground HF communications.
03System employed for air-ground communications including intercommunications and announcing system of aircraft.
04Radio-navigation Aids, operation of microphones and headphones, squelch, AVC, Volume control, tuning of transmitter; simple and duplex operation; advantages and disadvantages of radio telephone communication; limitations of range due to frequency interference.
R/T Radio Telephony Procedures
▶
01General operating procedures: Transmitting technique, transmission of letters/numbers/time, standard words and phrases, call signs, establishment and continuation of communications, transfer of communications, clearance and read-back requirements, test procedures.
02General phraseology: Role of phraseologies and plain language in radiotelephony, level instructions, position reporting, flight plans.
03Aerodrome control – Aircraft: Departure information, engine starting, push-back, taxi instructions, take-off, traffic circuit, final approach, landing, go around, after landing, essential aerodrome information.
04General ATS surveillance service phraseology: Identification and vectoring, traffic information, avoiding action, secondary surveillance radar, radar assistance to aircraft with communication failure, alerting phraseologies.
05Aerodrome control – Vehicles: Movement instructions, crossing runways, towing aircraft.
06Approach control: IFR departures, VFR departures, IFR/VFR arrivals, vectors to final approach, surveillance radar approach, precision radar approach.
07Area control: Area control units, position information, level information, flights joining/leaving/crossing airways, holding enroute, ATS Surveillance, ADS, Oceanic control.
08Distress & urgency / Communications failure: Distress messages, aircraft in distress, imposition of silence, termination, urgency messages, emergency descent, communications failure.
09Meteorological & aerodrome info transmission: Runway Visual Range (RVR), runway surface conditions.
10Miscellaneous flight handling: SELCAL, fuel dumping, wake turbulence, wind shear, direction finding, ACAS manoeuvres.
